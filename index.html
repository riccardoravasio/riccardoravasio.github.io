Postdoctoral Scholar, University of Chicago

I am currently working with Arvind Murugan and Rama Ranganathan on molecular evolution, specifically of non-equilibrium error correction machinery across biology. I use both theoretical (non-equilibrium statistical physics, coarse-grained mechanistic models) and experimental tools (high-throughput molecular biology, lab evolution).

About

My first exposure to research has been with Monte Carlo simulations of disordered systems supervised by Giulio Biroli, characterising how glassy systems jump energy barriers while reaching their equilibrium state. In my PhD, under the guidance of Matthieu Wyart, I used networks of springs as models for protein mechanics and identified principles for the emergence of long-range response in proteins. This action at a distance is the allosteric regulation ubiquitous across proteins and our work found general architectural features suggesting how it can be evolved and designed. The exposure to experimental data during my PhD has been mostly with protein crystallography data and with my postdoc I wanted to move closer to evolution and wet lab. The collaborative environment at UChicago gave me an unique oportunity to learn how to clone libraries and perform laboratory evolution and I have been working with Arvind Murugan and Rama Ranganathan on molecular evolution, from the evolution of error correction, as proofreading in DNA polymerases, to how easy is for a protein to get to perform new functions. Science aside, I enjoy gardening, ceramics, cooking, photography, food and a nice group of friends.
