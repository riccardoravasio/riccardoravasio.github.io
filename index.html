Postdoctoral Scholar, University of Chicago

I am currently working with Arvind Murugan and Rama Ranganathan on molecular evolution, specifically of non-equilibrium error correction machinery across biology. I use both theoretical (non-equilibrium statistical physics, coarse-grained mechanistic models) and experimental tools (high-throughput molecular biology and lab evolution).

About

My first exposure to research has been with Monte Carlo simulations of disordered systems supervised by Giulio Biroli, characterising how glassy systems jump energy barriers while reaching their equilibrium state. In my PhD, under the guidance of Matthieu Wyart, I used networks of springs as models for protein mechanics and identified principles for the emergence of long-range response in proteins. This action at a distance is the same allosteric regulation ubiquitous across proteins and our work. The exposure to experimental data with protein crystallography data was not enough
